### ğŸªµ Fireplace & Wood {#fireplace}

Those who live on each floor are responsible for the fireplace on their floor. **One of the things we do is that everyone after waking up gets one box of firewood outside.** Please ask whoever is around at the time what firewood is dry and can be used.

#### How to make a fire
- Take 2 logs and put them flush to the floor, leave a little room so air can get in (using lighter wood in the beginning is easier with some paper)
- Put a good amount of little sticks or bark on top, stack them 2-3 layers and leave room for air flow
- Set fire to wood wool covered with wax and put it between or on top of the twigs, or use a piece of paper.
- Open the ashtray door a little and get the oven as hot as possible with the first round of wood

#### Keeping the fire alive
- Open the door mostly when there are no more flames, take a metal tool and move the embers around (ash falls down and airflow is better)
- You can also put logs on while the fire is on!
- Put 1 or 2 logs in, use the heavier wood (it will burn for longer)
- Donâ€™t worry if there is only little embers left, itâ€™ll catch fire so long the oven is hot still

#### Learnings
- The wood should burn cleanly, without smoke
- If you see black soot building up on the glass and walls, try to raise the temperature by increasing the air flow: Caution, all the handles are too hot to be touched, use a non-plastic piece of cloth! 1. turn the handle on the pipe to a straight position (in the same direction as the pipe, so vertical in this case); 2. Increase the number on the dial behind the cover under the chamber on the left (use the lower scale)
- Donâ€™t over-adjust the airflow regulator. Donâ€™t try to burn the wood slowly, it can produce toxic gases. Starving the fire of air can lead to flash fires (gas detonations) and fire in the chimney. The flames should always be bright and friendly
- Pinewood (soft): 1, pipe valve diagonal
- Hardwood: 2, pipe valve vertical
